## Proposal 1 — Capsule prizes are tradable in the doc that implements PA-001, and PA-001 says they must not be

**Rank:** 1 (highest player impact × confidence ÷ blast radius — this is a correction, not a new
design decision: the owner already ruled it.)

**Owning doc:** `docs/10_systems/GACHAPON.md` §1.5 / §7 + its Open Questions.
Secondary: `docs/10_systems/ECONOMY.md` §6 (one bullet), `docs/10_systems/social/TRADING.md` §4
(the pending schema field).

### Problem

`docs/10_systems/MONETIZATION.md` line 21 — the PA-001 amendment text itself, the authority for
the whole gacha exception — reads:

> §2.2 and §2.5 apply to the capsule unchanged (prizes vendor at 0 **and are never tradable**;
> no real-money↔`shards` path).

`GACHAPON.md`, the owner doc that implements PA-001, never states this. It asks for exactly one
guardrail, in §7 and again in its Open Questions:

> **Vendor value 0 on everything dispensed.** Prizes are marked non-vendorable at dispense time
> — this needs a per-instance `no_vendor` flag on inventory items […]

and §1.5 justifies the whole containment claim on that single flag:

> every dispensed prize has **vendor value 0** (§7) — money can never become `shards`, so
> `10_systems/ECONOMY.md` §1's faucet law is untouched.

`no_vendor` is strictly weaker than what PA-001 requires. Non-vendorable is not untradable:

- 42% of all pulls are ordinary tradable inventory items — gear-modification scrolls (10%),
  emberstone caches (20%), equipment rolls ≤ `rare` (12%) per GACHAPON §5.
- `social/MARKET.md` "Reuses `TRADING.md`'s untradeable policy: quest items never list; boss
  uniques can." Capsule prizes are in neither excluded class, so they list.
- `social/TRADING.md` §4's Open Question confirms the enforcement field does not exist yet:
  "The concrete `tradeable`/`untradeable` field belongs on the item schema (Phase C […])" —
  still open. Nothing in the tree would catch the omission.

So the live path is: buy tickets → pull scrolls/stones/gear → list on the market → receive
`shards`. Real money becomes `shards`, and PA-001's §1.5 cap is defeated — not by a vendor, by a
player. `ECONOMY.md` §6's bullet has the same gap: it justifies "no path into the `shards`
supply" on vendor-0 alone. That is true of the *shard supply* (a market sale is a transfer, not a
faucet) but false of the thing PA-001 actually protects: whether money converts into in-world
buying power.

**Second, separable half — the weekly cap is 4× looser than stated.** GACHAPON §1.3 caps
purchases at "10 tickets **per character** per week", and its Open Question defers the
account-wide question to "once accounts exist". Accounts landed the *same day* as PA-001:
`ACCOUNTS_AUTH.md` §2.2 — "An account has **4 character slots at launch** (owner directive
2026-07-24)". The precondition in that Open Question is already met, so the effective bound is
**40 tickets/week/account**. Combined with the tradability gap above, all 40 funnel onto one main.

### Proposed change

1. **(Correction, no new decision.)** Widen the owed flag in GACHAPON §7 and its Open Question
   from `no_vendor` to bind-on-dispense: **no vendor, no trade, no market listing** — matching
   PA-001's wording. This adds no new mechanism: `TRADING.md` §4 already owes a
   `tradeable`/`untradeable` item-schema field at Phase C; capsule prizes simply need to set it.
   Amend GACHAPON §1.5 to rest the no-bridge claim on *both* properties, not vendor-0 alone.
2. **(Correction.)** Extend `ECONOMY.md` §6's Cogwork Capsule bullet the same way.
3. **(Owner call.)** Make the §1.3 weekly purchase cap **account-wide**, closing the alt-roster
   multiplier. Per PA-001's own text, changing a §1 cap is a new owner amendment, not a tune —
   hence flagged, not assumed.

### Why it helps

Directly defends the anti-pillar the owner accepted PA-001 *under*: "hard no-pay-to-win"
(`PILLARS.md` anti-pillars; MONETIZATION §2). Left as written, the tree's implementing doc ships
a guardrail the amendment already rejected, and Phase C would mint the weaker flag in good faith.
Also a Law-2 defect: two docs disagree on a rule, and the non-owner doc is the one builders read.

### Blast radius

**Small.** Items 1–2 are wording changes in two sections plus two Open-Question lines; no schema,
content, ID, or validator change (the Phase-C field is already owed and unauthored, so nothing is
retrofitted). Item 3 is one number's scope in GACHAPON §1.3. No change-controlled file is touched.
MARKET/TRADING are server-deferred stubs, so there is no live behavior to migrate — this is
exactly the cheap moment to fix it.

### Needs owner sign-off?

Items 1–2: **no** — they restore GACHAPON to what PA-001 already says. Item 3: **yes** — a §1 cap
change is a pillar amendment by PA-001's own terms.

### Verbatim Open-Questions text

For `docs/10_systems/GACHAPON.md` — replace the existing `no_vendor` bullet with:

```
- The per-instance bind-on-dispense flag (§7) does not yet exist in `20_schemas/item.schema.md` /
  `10_systems/INVENTORY.md` — owed at Phase C. PA-001 (`10_systems/MONETIZATION.md`) requires
  dispensed prizes to be **non-vendorable AND never tradable/listable**, not merely vendor-0:
  scrolls, emberstones and equip rolls are otherwise ordinary tradable items, and
  `10_systems/social/MARKET.md` would let a paying player convert them to `shards`, defeating

  (set false at dispense) plus the vendor flag. Cosmetics need no flag (non-item unlock entries,

- **Weekly purchase cap scope (§1.3) — the deferral condition is now met.** The cap is written
  per character, but `70_integrations/ACCOUNTS_AUTH.md` §2.2 grants **4 character slots at

  4× the intended bound. Owner call: make the cap account-wide. Changing a §1 cap is a new
  pillar amendment per PA-001, not a D-gate tune.
```

For `docs/10_systems/social/TRADING.md` — append to §4's existing schema-field question:

```

  requires them untradeable/unlistable at dispense, so the field must support a per-instance
  (not per-item-definition) value.
```

## Proposal 2 — A ~54-hour raid drought at Lv 56–69, the longest gap in the game

**Rank:** 2 (largest player-experience hole found; bounded fix exists that mints no map or mob IDs.)

**Owning doc:** `docs/10_systems/social/RAID.md` §2 (the band table).
Secondary: `docs/WORLD_PLAN.md` §Raids, `docs/10_systems/LEVELING.md` §3.1.

### Problem

`RAID.md` §3 gates raids with a **hard level band at both ends**, checked at instance creation.
The four bands are 15–22, 32–40, 45–55, 70–80. That leaves three windows with no raid available.
Measured in `/played` against the ratified LEVELING §1 table:

| Drought window | `/played` spent with no raid in band |
|---|---|
| Lv 23–31 | ≈ 9.6 h (interpolated from the §1 table) |
| Lv 41–44 | ≈ 9.7 h (interpolated) |
| **Lv 56–69** | **≈ 53.7 h** (cum 118.2 h @ Lv 70 − cum 64.5 h @ Lv 55, both table rows) |

The third window is **5.5× either of the others**, and ≈ **1.6× the entire Arc 1** (Lv 1→42 ≈
33.6 h, LEVELING §1.1). It covers essentially all of Arcane Reach — the only one of 11 regions
with no raid and no raid-band overlap whatsoever.

What goes dark for those 54 hours is not a side activity. Per `RAID.md` §6, raids are "the only
source of `raid_token`-gated exclusive rewards", and §6.D's first-clear-of-the-day bonus is
explicitly designed as "the 'log in and raid with the guild' social rhythm" with no weekly
lockout. For the single longest stretch of the game, a player has no group content beat, no token
faucet, and no reason to form a party beyond field hunting.

Supporting evidence — the same window is where content novelty collapses hardest:

| | Maps | `/played` | Maps per hour | New monsters per hour |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arc 1 (R1–R8) | 200 | ≈ 33.6 h | 5.95 | 4.46 (`mob_001`–`150`) |
| Arc 2 (R9–R11) | 124 | ≈ 136.6 h | 0.91 | 0.61 (`mob_151`–`234`) |

A **6.5× drop in new-map rate and 7.3× in new-monster rate**, and the raid drought sits in the
middle of it. Arc 2 carries 38% of the world's maps across 80% of its playtime.

### Proposed change

Two options; both close the window. I recommend **A**.

**A — a fifth raid in Arcane Reach, reusing maps that already exist.** Arcane Reach already has
the exact structure every other raid uses: a 3-map dungeon chain (`map_277`–`279`, the Shattered
Orrery) and a region arena with a boss (`map_284`, the Anchor Vault; Aetheron, `mob_206`, Lv 68).
That is byte-for-byte the `raid_mainspring` / `raid_deepfrost` / `raid_voidtide` layout, so a raid
at band ≈ 56–68 mints **zero new map IDs and zero new mob IDs**, and it honors WORLD_PLAN's
"each ends at an existing boss arena (no extra boss slots)".

Honest cost, stated in full: a new `raid_<name>` GLOSSARY token, a `raid_token` variant in
ID_REGISTRY, a `RAID.md` §2 row, a `LEVELING.md` §3.1 exp row, a WORLD_PLAN raid entry, and the
"4 raids" headline updated in README / CLAUDE.md / SCOPE / WORLD_PLAN. One real content cost:
R9 and R11 each reserved 2 quests for raid intro/handler (`099`–`100`, `119`–`120`), but **R10's
block `101`–`110` reserved none** — so two quests must be repurposed from that block or the block
extended in a new commit (Law 3: extend, never renumber).

**B — widen the two neighboring bands** (e.g. `raid_deepfrost` 45–62, `raid_voidtide` 62–80).
Two number edits in `RAID.md` §2, zero content. Cheaper, but it degrades both encounters: a Lv-62
party in a Lv-55 raid trivializes it, and RAID §3's hard both-ends gate exists precisely to keep
raid difficulty matched to the party. It converts a content gap into a balance problem.

### Why it helps

P6 ("Multiplayer-shaped from day one") — the social spine currently goes dark for the longest
single stretch of the authored game. P2 ("the grind is cozy, not cruel"; "sessions of 20 minutes
must feel rewarding") — Lv 56–69 is where per-level cost passes 3 hours and novelty per hour is at
its lowest, so it is the worst possible place to also remove the group beat.

### Blast radius

Option A: **medium.** No map/mob IDs minted and no change-controlled file touched, but it moves a
headline count ("4 raids") that appears in six places, and it needs two quest IDs. Option B:
**small** but with a real balance cost. Either way, no validator or schema change.

### Needs owner sign-off?

**Yes** — both options change a ratified world-structure headline (the raid count or the band
table). This is a proposal for a decision, not a correction.

### Verbatim Open-Questions text

For `docs/10_systems/social/RAID.md`:

```
- **Raid-band coverage gap at Lv 56–69 (≈ 53.7 `/played` hours).** The §2 bands (15–22, 32–40,
  45–55, 70–80) plus §3's hard both-ends gate leave three windows with no raid available: Lv
  23–31 (≈ 9.6 h), Lv 41–44 (≈ 9.7 h), and Lv 56–69 (≈ 53.7 h — `10_systems/LEVELING.md` §1 cum
  118.2 h @ Lv 70 − 64.5 h @ Lv 55). The third is 5.5× either other window and ≈ 1.6× the whole
  of Arc 1, and covers all of Arcane Reach — the only region with no raid band overlapping it. For
  that stretch there is no `raid_token` faucet and no §6.D daily group beat. Owner call:
  (A) add a fifth raid at ≈ 56–68 reusing `map_277`–`279` → `map_284` (Aetheron, `mob_206`) —
  mints no map or mob IDs, but moves the "4 raids" headline and needs 2 quest IDs (R10's
  `101`–`110` block reserved none); or (B) widen `raid_deepfrost` / `raid_voidtide` bands to
  meet, which is two edits but puts over-leveled parties in under-leveled raids. Owner:
  this doc with `docs/WORLD_PLAN.md` + `10_systems/LEVELING.md` §3.1.
```

## Proposal 3 — The Gilded Charter fee is ~16 minutes of income, so the two-lane structure carries no decision

**Rank:** 3 (high confidence, one-number fix, but the number is already marked first-pass —
what is new here is that nobody has computed what it does to the *structure*.)

**Owning doc:** `docs/10_systems/ECONOMY.md` §4.4 (owns the number).
Secondary: `docs/10_systems/BATTLE_PASS.md` §1/§6 (owns everything else about the charter).

### Problem

ECONOMY §4.4 prices the gilt unlock at a flat **6,000 `shards` per character per 30-day season**,
and states it is "≈ 16 minutes of Lv 30 at-level income […] a real monthly sink that stays cozy".
The first half checks out against §5's own table; the second does not follow from it.

Against §5's modeled income (gross / net of the tonic bite):

| Player Lv | Net income/hr (§5) | 6,000 `shards` = |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | 7,560 | ≈ 48 min |
| 30 | 14,040 | ≈ 26 min |
| 50 | 15,840 | ≈ 23 min |
| 70 | 24,840 | ≈ 14 min |

The sharpest comparison is inside the same table: at Lv 50 a player spends **21,600 `shards`/hour
on tonics**, so the entire premium lane of a 30-day season costs **less than 17 minutes of
potions**. Over a month at even 1 h/day at Lv 50, gilt is ≈ **1.3% of net income**.

Two consequences:

1. **The choice is not a choice.** Every player past roughly Lv 20 buys gilt in the first minutes
   of every season. The free lane is then content nobody experiences on its own, and
   BATTLE_PASS's carefully-built premium framing — "**Cosmetics are the gilt lane's headline
   offer**" (§5.1, owner decision 2026-07-24) — describes cosmetics that are effectively free
   with an extra click. The design spends real complexity on that distinction: a second lane, a
   `charter_gilt` token, retroactive purchase, the §5.2 power-parity rule, per-character
   per-season purchase state, and claim UI. The mechanism costs more than the decision it creates.
2. **It is the only unscaled sink, and ECONOMY's own guardrail says sinks must scale.** §6's
   first inflation guardrail reads "**Sinks scale with level.** Enhancement fee […], tonic price
   […], reallocation fee […] all rise, so a high-level player's `shards` outflow grows with their
   income". Gilt is flat, so its real cost decays from ≈ 48 min at Lv 10 to ≈ 14 min at Lv 70 and
   keeps falling — the exact drift §6 exists to prevent. This is an internal contradiction, not
   an outside opinion.

### Proposed change

Do **not** simply raise the flat number — §4.4 already notes it is ≈ 42 minutes at Lv 10, so any
raise large enough to matter at Lv 50 is punishing at Lv 10. Instead, pick one of:

- **(recommended) Band-scale the gilt fee**, like every other ECONOMY sink, so it stays a
  constant fraction of a season's modeled net income at the player's band. That makes it a real
  monthly decision at every level without a low-level wall, and it self-corrects when the balance
  pass moves income. The magnitude is the owner's — I am deliberately not inventing one.
- **Or accept it as a courtesy unlock and collapse to one lane**, deleting the gilt machinery
  (token, purchase state, retroactive-claim rule, §5.2's separate parity clause) and simply
  gating the cosmetics behind charter level. Fewer moving parts, same player experience.

Either is coherent. The current shape is the one that is not.

### Why it helps

P2 ("cozy, not cruel", and the charter's own §1 "Cozy, not FOMO") is preserved by both options —
neither adds pressure. What is gained is honesty about the structure: either the gilt lane is a
decision and should cost like one, or it is not and should not carry a decision's machinery.
Also serves ECONOMY §6's stated anti-inflation intent, which the flat fee currently undercuts.

### Blast radius

**Small.** Option 1 is one price rule in ECONOMY §4.4 plus a citation refresh in BATTLE_PASS §6;
both docs already flag the number as first-pass pending the D-gate balance pass, so this lands
inside planned work. Option 2 is larger (removes sections from BATTLE_PASS) but strictly deletes.
No content, IDs, schemas, or change-controlled files.

### Needs owner sign-off?

**Yes** — the two-lane structure was explicitly owner-approved on 2026-07-24 (BATTLE_PASS Open
Questions, "feature approved as designed"). Changing what the lane costs, or collapsing it, is the
owner's call. The arithmetic is not.

### Verbatim Open-Questions text

For `docs/10_systems/ECONOMY.md`:

```
- **Gilt charter fee (§4.4) is flat, and §6's first guardrail says sinks must scale.** At 6,000
  `shards` the fee is ≈ 48 min of net income at Lv 10 but ≈ 14 min at Lv 70 (§5 net column), and
  ≈ 1.3% of a month's net income at Lv 50 — less than 17 minutes of that band's tonic spend. Every
  player therefore buys gilt immediately, so `10_systems/BATTLE_PASS.md`'s two-lane structure
  carries no decision while still costing a `charter_gilt` token, retroactive-purchase rules,
  §5.2's parity clause, and per-character season state. Owner call at the D-gate balance pass:
  band-scale the fee like every other sink in §6 (magnitude owner-set), or accept it as a
  courtesy unlock and collapse the charter to one lane. Owner: this doc with
  `10_systems/BATTLE_PASS.md`.
```

For `docs/10_systems/BATTLE_PASS.md` — replace the fee clause in its existing "All numbers […]
are first-pass" question with a pointer:

```
- The 6,000-`shards` gilt fee (`10_systems/ECONOMY.md` §4.4) is not merely first-pass but
  structurally load-bearing: at ≈ 1.3% of a month's net income it makes §5's free/gilt split a
  formality. Flagged in ECONOMY §4.4's Open Questions; resolve there before the season schema
  (§5.1) and `season_001` content are authored.
```

## Watchlist for next pass

1. **Arc-2 content density.** 38% of the world's maps carry 80% of its playtime (6.5× fewer new
   maps/hour than Arc 1, 7.3× fewer new monsters/hour). The ratified LEVELING anchors are owner
   decisions and I am not proposing to overturn them; Proposal 2 addresses the actionable slice.
   Worth watching whether the balance pass adds any *repeatable* Arc-2 content beyond two raids.
2. **Combo × spec interaction.** `COMBO_SYSTEM.md` grows momentum only on *source change* within
   a 3.0 s window, so sustaining a 6- or 10-link chain requires several low-cooldown actives.
   Unverified this pass: whether the deliberate single-target specs (Sureshot, Duskstep, JOBS
   §7.1) can reach tier III at all. If not, the ×1.15 tier is silently spec-gated — a dominant
   strategy hiding inside a balance multiplier. Needs a read of the five skill content files.
3. **"The game's single real-money product."** CLAUDE.md and GACHAPON call the capsule ticket the
   only real-money SKU, while MONETIZATION §3.1/§4 still describe a future `gleam`-priced cosmetic
   store. Not a contradiction today (PA-001 bounds the *exception*, not the future store), but the
   wording will collide the moment that store is designed. Low priority.
4. **Whether the `no_vendor`/untradeable correction (Proposal 1) landed** before Phase C mints the
   item-schema flag. After that point it stops being a wording fix.
5. `MAP_INTERACTABLES.md` still owes a `capsule` interactable kind (GACHAPON §2, already flagged
   there) — mechanical, tracked, no action needed from this review.
