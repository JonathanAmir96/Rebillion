Rebillion — Combat & Combo Layer Mock-up

The skill-chaining layer in motion: combo_momentum links, the 3.0 s chain window, momentum tiers, and the combo_burst finisher — rules owned by COMBO_SYSTEM.md, drawing owned by HUD.md §7/§7.1, folded into the damage pipeline at COMBAT_FORMULA.md §2 step 8. Sample character: Ember, Lv 24 flicker (1st job — momentum caps at tier II, COMBO_SYSTEM §3). Skills are real content: skill_flicker_001 Twin Fangs, skill_flicker_003 Throwing Dirk. Sprites/hex values are illustrative placeholders — art identity is owned by ART_BIBLE.yaml. Gold numbered pins are design annotations, not game UI.

▶ Everything on this page runs on one shared 8-second rotation clock, so the counter, window bar, gauges, and timeline below always agree: basic → Twin Fangs → Throwing Dirk (burst) → basic → Twin Fangs → Throwing Dirk (burst on cooldown) → window expires → reset. Motion is honored per prefers-reduced-motion; with reduced motion the page settles on the end-of-rotation frame.

4 combo_burst: punch-scale flash reusing the §7 crit language, ×1.25 on every instance of that cast COMBO_SYSTEM §4, HUD §7.1
5 Support/mobility casts are neutral — they neither grow, refresh, nor break the chain COMBO_SYSTEM §2.3
6 Burst internal cooldown 8 s — the second finisher at t=5.0 meets the pattern but cannot burst COMBO_SYSTEM §4
7 Burst refunds 5% of max essence — a flat kickback, not a restore_essence op COMBO_SYSTEM §4

Asymmetric by design: monsters have no combo layer, the same asymmetry as player i-frames. combo_momentum is not a status — it never occupies a slot in the 12-slot status row (STATUS_EFFECTS §1), has no icon, and cannot be cleansed or dispelled.

2 · The rotation on a ruler (what the clock above is doing)

The counter grows only on a source change (COMBO_SYSTEM §2.2): mashing basic attack, or one skill, holds the chain at 1 link forever. A same-source link still refreshes the window — it just adds nothing. A multi-hit or multi-target cast is one link: an aoe_circle clearing a pack of eight counts once (§2.1).

4 · Where the multiplier actually lands (COMBAT_FORMULA.md §2)

inside COMBAT_FORMULA §15's rotation factor mult m. A non-comboer clears an at-level normal in ≈5.2 s, still inside the §14 3–6 s TTK band.

This is the whole containment argument: combos are a damage-per-minute edge, never a gate. If a balance pass retunes the magnitudes (window, thresholds, multipliers, burst ICD), it retunes them against mult m — never against the §14 TTK bands.
