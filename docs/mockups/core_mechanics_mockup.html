Rebillion — Core Mechanics Mock-up

The four systems a player feels every minute, drawn with their real numbers: traversal (MAP_TRAVERSAL.md), elements (ELEMENTS.md), status effects (STATUS_EFFECTS.md, HUD §8), and the i-frame / hitstun contract (COMBAT_FORMULA §11–§12). One tile = 16 px; the tile grid is the measurement and validation lens, not the terrain — walkable ground is a chain of foothold segments with painted terrain_chunk art snapped to them (AB-001). Sprites/hex values are illustrative placeholders — art identity is owned by ART_BIBLE.yaml. Gold numbered pins are design annotations, not game UI.

▶ The traversal run below loops on a 16-second clock and touches every movement mechanic in the design, in order. Motion is honored per prefers-reduced-motion.

1 · One run through every traversal mechanic (MAP_TRAVERSAL.md)

1 Walkable ground is a chain of foothold segments of arbitrary angle — organic ground is not built from axis-aligned tiles MAP_TRAVERSAL §1, AB-001
2 Jump budget: ≤5 tiles horizontal, ≤3.5 tiles rise — each a single-axis maximum, never an additive corner. Author ≤70% of either when the other is non-trivial MAP_TRAVERSAL §1.1
3 One-way (layer 2): solid from above, passable from below and the sides. Drop-through suspends collision for 0.3 s — long enough to fall clear, too short to hold as a stealth mode MAP_TRAVERSAL §3
4 Ropes and ladders are the same mechanic with different dressing — one climb state, implemented once. Jump off = a fixed 1.5-tile hop MAP_TRAVERSAL §4
5 Hazards deal a flat % of max life — not mitigated by armor/warding, not element-multiplied, never knockback. A player who dies to spikes always knows why MAP_TRAVERSAL §6
6 A moving platform is ordinary terrain plus a motion behavior — motion is orthogonal to the collision layer, not a new one MAP_TRAVERSAL §5
7 Falling never damages the player, from any height. Height is a level-design tool, not a threat MAP_TRAVERSAL §2

Hazard tiers: minor 5% (shallow spikes, thorns) · standard 10% (pit spikes, hot coals) · severe 20% (lava, active chasms). Reapplication is gated by the player's 0.40 s i-frames, so continuous contact cannot deal damage faster than ≈ once per 0.40 s — the same throttle that prevents monster-touch stun-lock.

2 · Elements — per-monster affinity lists, deliberately not a wheel (ELEMENTS.md)

skill's declared element, even when a physical weapon carries an elemental skill. why no wheel Affinities are per-monster so a player learns "this beetle hates fire" from play and telegraph, not from memorizing a global table. Authoring guide: 0–2 weak_to, 0–2 resists, rarely an immune_to. A monster with no lists is plain ×1.0.

3 · Status effects — the 12-slot row and its hard rules (STATUS_EFFECTS §1, HUD §8)

hard per-entity status cap, so no overflow or scroll handling exists anywhere. If a 13th would apply, the active status with the least remaining duration is dropped to admit it (ties: oldest applied). Scripted boss statuses may be flagged non-displaceable. combo_momentum never appears here — it is combat-rhythm state, not a status: no icon, no slot, uncleansable. hard-CC diminishing returns on a single target — the anti-chain-stun rule 1st stun — full duration 2nd — 50% 3rd within 10 s → CC-immune for 8 s soft CC (chill/root/silence/blind) has no immunity DR

Damage-over-time is snapshot, not dynamic: a DoT captures the applier's source_power and crit_power at application and every tick uses that snapshot — including the combo_momentum that was active at the moment of application. Refreshing re-snapshots; each independent stack carries its own. regen is the exception: it reads the receiver's current max life live, because it is a heal, not a snapshotted hit.

4 · The hit contract — hitstun, knockback, and the 0.40 s i-frame window

and status applications ✓ also attaches to dash/leap skills that opt in (iframes: true, default false) ✗ does not cleanse DoTs already on the player — burn and poison keep ticking ✗ does not apply between ticks of one multi-hit player skill Monsters get no i-frames. That asymmetry is deliberate — it is what lets player combos land in full. The window also bounds monster touch stacking at ≈ one instance per 0.8 s of overlap: no stun-lock deaths. Death stings, it never deletes an evening (P2). The i-frame window, the no-fall-damage rule, the hazard throttle, and the CC immunity cap are four separate expressions of that same pillar — each one caps how fast a bad situation can compound while you are trying to recover from it.
