What a monster is from the player's side of the screen: the tier readouts (HUD §6/§6.1), the shared 8-state machine every profile walks (AI_BEHAVIOR §1), aggro/leash geometry (§2), the telegraph fairness floors (ANIMATION_TIMING §4), and the 12 behavior profiles as animated vignettes. Monsters quoted are real Phase D content — mob_001 Tuftmoth Grub, mob_011 Embermane Alpha, mob_027 The Cellar King. Sprites/hex values are illustrative placeholders — art identity is owned by ART_BIBLE.yaml. Gold numbered pins are design annotations, not game UI.

▶ Every vignette on this page loops. Cyan dashed shapes are design instruments (radii, bands, timers) the player never sees; the only things a player actually sees are the sprite, its animation, and the HUD readouts. Motion is honored per prefers-reduced-motion.

1 · Three tiers, three readouts (what the player is told, and when)

1 Normal: life bar beneath the sprite, show-on-damage, 4 s fade; a mob you have not hit shows nothing HUD §6.1
2 Elite: the same bar with framed trim, name tag in ember.light, and a mandatory telegraph on every windup HUD §6.1, AI_BEHAVIOR §2
3 Boss: top-center boss_bar with phase pips and no numeric readout — you read the fight, not the number HUD §6

Entering die exits the state machine from any state and clears every status immediately — no post-mortem DoT ticks, no kill-then-tick (STATUS_EFFECTS §1, ANIMATION_STATES).

2 · The shared 8-state machine every profile walks (AI_BEHAVIOR §1)

Not every profile uses all eight — each profile's entry names its own subset. stationary_turret never leaves idle/windup/attack/recover; passive_wanderer only ever reaches flee, and only after being struck.

3 · Aggro, the vertical band, and the leash — the platformer geometry

A pack_hunter's call pulls allies within pack_call_radius, but each called member still leashes from its own home point, never the caller's — a pack can only be pulled as far as its individual members' leashes allow. That single rule is what stops train-pulling.
