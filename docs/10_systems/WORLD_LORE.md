## 1. The Kiln Age (Emberfoot Isle)
Emberfoot Village was founded on a shallow vein of emberstone (`GLOSSARY.md`), the same
material `10_systems/ENHANCEMENT.md` still uses today. Its kilns forged tools and simple arms
for generations of settlers before the vein thinned. When the ember-fire dimmed, the smiths
banked the last kiln rather than let it go fully cold — and something in the embers kept
breathing. Cindermaw, the furnace-hound, is what the banked kiln became: not built, not
tamed, just left to smolder until it woke. The Old Kiln Tunnels beneath the village are the
mine workings that followed the vein; they are sealed at the far end, not exhausted.

## 2. Harthmoor Before the Ring
Harthmoor Isle's ring towns (Millbrook, Mossmere, Cindershelf, Tidewatch Port, Rosen Harbor)
were not built around the Clockwork Ruins by design — they grew there because the ruins were
already old and already still when the first Harthmoor settlers arrived. Brass and gearwork
salvage from the ruins' outer fields seeded Millbrook's early trade; the ring road itself
follows the safe walking distance early surveyors kept from the center. No living memory
holds what the ruins were called before "Clockwork."

## 3. The Clockwork Civilization
A single vast engine-city once ran on one driving mechanism: the Mainspring, at its heart.
Every gearworks, gate, and warden-engine in the ruins wound from that one spring, and its
citizens lived by its tick. The city built The Custodian and its kin to keep the gates, tend
the gearworks, and mind the streets — service, not conquest, was the Custodian's whole
purpose.

## 4. The Long Unwinding
The Mainspring's driving force began to fail — slowly, over generations, not in one collapse.
As the tick slowed, district by district lost power, light, and warmth, and the citizens left
for Harthmoor's open coast rather than freeze in the dark gearworks. The evacuation was
orderly enough that the wardens were never given a stand-down order. The Custodian and its
kin are still ticking through duties for citizens who left centuries ago, waiting on an
order that will not come. `raid_mainspring`'s trial threads the last chambers around the
spring itself.

## 5. The Drowned Kingdom (Sunken Depths)
Long before Harthmoor's ring towns, a seafaring kingdom held the coast where Tidewatch now
stands. Its capital sank when the shelf beneath it gave way — whether storm, subsidence, or
something else is lost with it. The Drowned Warden was that kingdom's last gatekeeper, posted
at its final door and never relieved; it keeps that post still, in the bioluminescent gloom
below the coast, guarding a threshold whose far side no living soul has seen. `raid_undervault`
and the Cellar King's flooded grain-stores are a smaller, later echo of the same coastline
giving way beneath Millbrook.

## 6. Where the Wild Things Come From
Gloomwood was once a healthy dark-canopy continuation of Verdant Hollow. Mother Gloam's brood
spread a webbing gloom outward from a single hollow tree until the whole wood lost its light;
whether she caused the dark or merely thrived first in it is unknown (see §7). Ashfall
Barrens burns because Emberfoot's ember-vein has a sister seam running under Harthmoor's
north crown; Karnothal, the Stoker, is a colossus of that seam's slag, endlessly feeding
cinders that keep the dunes hot and the spires charred.

## 7. Deliberately Unanswered (future-arc space — do not elaborate in content)
- What actually broke the Mainspring's driving force.
- Where the Clockwork citizens' descendants ended up after the long walk to the coast.
- The drowned kingdom's name, and what lies behind the Warden's last door.
- Whether Mother Gloam caused Gloomwood's dark or was drawn to it.
- What lies beyond the five charted islands — only the `rift` biome (and the four 3rd jobs)
  remains reserved future-space (`GLOSSARY.md`; `WORLD_PLAN.md` Open Questions), and this
  file does not name what, if anything, waits there.

**Note (factual, v3.1):** Frostpeak Isle (`frostpeak`), Arcane Reach (`arcane_reach`), and
Voidshore (`voidshore`) are built Arc-2 isles, no longer unanswered space — but their lore,
along with the Deepway passage's and the Harthmoor Longship Line's, is not yet written in
this primer. Until it is, Arc-2 flavor text may reference only `docs/WORLD_PLAN.md`'s
region-section facts (see Open Questions).

## Open Questions
- Whether the Drowned Kingdom and the Clockwork civilization are the same people at two
  different eras (coastal capital vs. inland engine-city) or entirely unrelated is not fixed
  here; flavor text should treat them as unrelated until an owner resolves it.
- Whether `raid_undervault`'s flooded stores are literally connected to Sunken Depths' water
  table (one drowning, one system) or merely a thematic echo is unresolved; content should
  not assert a literal tunnel between them without this being settled.
- Arc-2 lore is unwritten (§7's note): this primer needs sections for Frostpeak Isle, Arcane
  Reach, Voidshore, the Deepway, and the longship line before their flavor text can reference
  anything beyond `docs/WORLD_PLAN.md`'s region-section facts. Owner: this doc's owner
  (`docs/60_agents/roles/ROLE_NARRATIVE_WRITER.md`).
